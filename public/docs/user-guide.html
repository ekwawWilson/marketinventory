Chapter 1

Getting Started — Logging In

How to open the app and get into your account.

🔐 How to Log In

  1. Open your browser (Chrome or Firefox) and go to the app website.

  2. You will see a Login page. Type your Email Address.

  3. Type your Password.

  4. Click the blue "Sign In" button.

  5. You will land on the Dashboard — the main home page of the app.

💡 Tip: If you forget your password, ask the business owner (Admin) to reset it for you.

🏠 Understanding the Screen

📋

Left Side Menu (Sidebar)

All the sections of the app — Sales, Stock, Reports, etc. Click any item to go there.

📊

Main Area

The big area in the middle shows your information — lists, forms, and totals.

📱

Bottom Menu (Phone)

On your phone, the menu appears at the bottom of the screen. Tap the icons to navigate.

👤

Your Name (Top Right)

Shows who is logged in. Click it to sign out when you are done.

Chapter 2

Adding Your Products (Items)

Before you can sell anything, you must add your goods into the system.

📦 Adding a New Product

  1. Click "Inventory" in the left menu, then click "Items".

  2. Click the "+ Add Item" button (top right).

  3. Fill in the form:

Field What to Type Example
Item Name The name of the product Bag of Sugar (50kg)
Manufacturer / Brand Who makes it (optional) Olam, Unilever
Cost Price How much YOU paid for it 280.00
Selling Price How much you sell it for 320.00
Quantity How many you have right now 25

  1. Click "Save Item". Done!

💡 Profit Helper: The app automatically shows you your profit margin as you type. A green number means you are making money. A red number means you are losing money — adjust your selling price!

⚠️ Stock Alerts — What the Colours Mean

When you look at your items list, you will see coloured labels:

✓ In Stock — You have enough goods.

⚠ Low Stock — Less than 10 units left. Time to restock soon.

✗ Out of Stock — You have none left. Restock immediately!

⚠ Watch Out: If a product is Out of Stock, you cannot sell it. Restock by recording a new purchase (Chapter 5).

✏️ Updating Stock or Price

  1. Go to Inventory → Items.

  2. Find the product you want to change. Click on its name.

  3. Click the "Edit" button.

  4. Change the price or quantity, then click "Save".

💡 Better way to add stock: Instead of editing, use "Adjust Stock" on the items page. This keeps a proper record of why stock changed.

Chapter 3

Recording a Sale

Every time you sell goods, record it here so your stock reduces automatically.

🧾 How to Record a Sale

  1. Click "Sales" in the left menu, then "New Sale".

  2. Customer (optional): If you know the customer, select their name from the list. If it is a stranger or cash buyer, leave this empty.

  3. Click "+ Add Item" to add what you are selling.

  4. Select the product name from the dropdown.

  5. Enter the quantity sold.

  6. The price fills in automatically. You can change it if you gave a discount.

  7. Add more items if the customer bought more than one thing.

  8. Enter how much the customer paid in the "Amount Paid" box.

  9. Choose Payment Method: Cash, MoMo, or Bank.

  10. Click "Record Sale".

💡 Paid less than the total? If the customer paid less than the full amount, that means they owe you money (credit). The system will record this as a debt automatically.

🖨️ After Recording — What Happens Next?

After saving a sale, you will see a green success screen with three choices:

🖨️

Print Receipt

Prints or saves a receipt to give to the customer.

➕

New Sale

Starts a new blank sale form for the next customer.

📋

View All Sales

Goes back to the full list of all sales you have made.

🔄

Process Return

If the customer wants to return something from this sale.

📋 Viewing Past Sales

  1. Click Sales → All Sales in the menu.

  2. You will see a list of all sales. The newest ones are at the top.

  3. Use the search box to find a specific customer or date.

  4. Click on any sale to see the full receipt and details.

Chapter 4

Quick Checkout with POS

The POS (Point of Sale) screen is a fast checkout till — perfect for busy markets.

🏪 What is the POS Screen?

The POS Terminal is a special full-screen checkout page. It is faster than the normal sale form and works like a till machine in a supermarket. You can scan barcodes or just tap/click items to add them to the basket.

💡 When to use POS: Use the POS for fast cash sales when customers are standing in front of you. Use the normal Sale Form (Chapter 3) for credit sales or when you need to record more details.

⚡ How to Use the POS

  1. Click "POS Terminal" in the left menu. The screen will go full size.

  2. Find your item — Type the item name in the search box at the top. The matching items appear below as big tiles.

  3. Click on an item tile to add it to the cart (basket) on the right.

  4. To change the quantity, click the item in the cart then use the number pad to type the correct amount.

  5. Select how the customer is paying — Cash 💵, MoMo 📱, or Bank 🏦.

  6. If paying cash, type how much the customer gave you in the "Cash Tendered" box. The change amount shows automatically.

  7. Click the big green "Pay & Checkout" button.

  8. Done! The screen flashes green and is ready for the next customer.

🗑️ Removing Items from the Cart

If you added the wrong item:

  1. Click on the item in the cart to select it (it gets highlighted).

  2. Press the delete (trash) button next to the item, or set the quantity to 0 using the number pad.

To clear everything and start fresh, click the "Clear Cart" button.

Chapter 5

Recording a Purchase (Buying Stock)

When you buy goods from a supplier, record it here so your stock increases automatically.

🚛 How to Record a Purchase

  1. Click "Purchasing" in the left menu, then "New Purchase".

  2. Select your Supplier from the list. (If they are not listed, add them first — see below.)

  3. Click "+ Add Item" to choose what you bought.

  4. Select the product name. If it is a new product, create it in Items first (Chapter 2).

  5. Enter the quantity you bought.

  6. Enter the cost price per unit (what you paid).

  7. Add more items if you bought multiple products from the same supplier.

  8. Enter how much you already paid the supplier.

  9. Click "Record Purchase".

💡 Paid less than the total? If you have not paid the supplier fully yet, type only what you paid. The remaining amount will show as money you owe the supplier.

✅ What Happens After?

  • Your stock for those products goes up automatically.
  • Your supplier balance is updated (if you did not pay in full).
  • The purchase is saved in Purchasing → All Purchases for your records.

👤 Adding a New Supplier

  1. Click "Purchasing" in the menu, then "Suppliers".

  2. Click "+ New Supplier".

  3. Enter the supplier's name, phone number, and any notes.

  4. Click "Save".

Now you can select this supplier when recording purchases.

Chapter 6

Managing Customers

Keep track of the people you sell to — especially those who owe you money.

👥 Adding a New Customer

  1. Click "CRM" in the menu, then "Customers".

  2. Click "+ New Customer".

  3. Enter their name and phone number.

  4. Click "Save".

💡 Why add customers? When you add customers, you can track who owes you money (credit sales), see their purchase history, and contact them later.

💰 Tracking Who Owes You (Debtors)

Go to CRM → Customers. You will see three tabs:

All — Shows every customer.

Debtors — Shows only customers who still owe you money.

Paid Up — Shows customers with no outstanding balance.

Click on any customer name to see their full history — all their sales and payments.

⚠ Important: A customer only appears in your sales form if they are added here first. For strangers who pay cash, you do not need to add them.

Chapter 7

Collecting Debt Payments

When a customer pays back money they owe you, record it here.

💵 Recording a Customer Payment

  1. Click "Finance" in the menu, then "Payments → Customers".

  2. Select the customer's name from the dropdown. Only customers who owe you money will appear here.

  3. Enter the amount they are paying today.

  4. Choose the payment method: Cash, MoMo, or Bank.

  5. Click "Record Payment".

💡 Shortcut: You can also go to CRM → Customers, click on the customer's name, and then click "Record Payment" directly from their profile page.

🏢 Paying a Supplier (Money You Owe)

  1. Click "Finance" in the menu, then "Payments → Suppliers".

  2. Select the supplier you are paying.

  3. Enter the amount and payment method.

  4. Click "Record Payment".

Chapter 8

Processing Returns

When a customer brings goods back, or when you return goods to a supplier.

🔄 Customer Return (Goods Brought Back to You)

  1. Click "Returns" in the left menu.

  2. Click the "Customer Return" button at the top.

  3. Enter the Sale ID (the sale number, shown on the receipt). Click "Look Up".

  4. Select the item being returned from the dropdown.

  5. Enter the quantity being returned.

  6. Choose the Return Type:

💵

Cash Refund

You give the customer their money back.

📋

Store Credit

The customer gets credit to buy something else later.

🔄

Exchange

The customer swaps the item for a different one.

—

 

 

  1. Enter the refund amount. (The app can calculate it for you — click the auto-fill button.)

  2. Click "Process Return". Done!

🚛 Supplier Return (You Send Goods Back to Supplier)

This works the same way, but use the "Supplier Return" button instead.

  1. Click "Returns" → "Supplier Return".

  2. Enter the Purchase ID from your purchase records.

  3. Select the item, quantity, return type, and amount.

  4. Click "Process Return".

💡 You can also process returns directly from a sale or purchase detail page — look for the orange "Process Return" button on the receipt.

Chapter 9

Checking Your Reports

See how your business is doing — daily, weekly, or any time period you choose.

📈 Sales Report

  1. Click "Reports" in the menu, then "Sales Report".

  2. Choose a start date and end date (e.g., the start and end of this week).

  3. The report will show you:

💰

Total Sales

All the money from sales in that period.

✅

Total Collected

Money you have actually received in hand.

⏳

Credit / Debt

Money that customers still owe you from sales.

📥

Export to Excel

Download the data as a spreadsheet file.

📦 Inventory Report

Go to Reports → Inventory Report. This shows:

  • What products you have and how many.
  • The total value of all your stock.
  • Which items are running low.

Use this every week to know what you need to restock.

📅 End of Day Report

Go to Reports → End of Day. Run this at the end of every trading day to see:

  • Total sales for the day.
  • Cash, MoMo, and Bank amounts collected.
  • Number of transactions completed.
💡 Good habit: Run the End of Day report every evening before closing your shop. Print or screenshot it for your daily records.

👥 Debtors & Creditors Reports

These reports show all money owed:

👥

Debtors Report

Lists all customers who still owe you money. Go to Reports → Debtors.

🏢

Creditors Report

Lists all suppliers you still owe money to. Go to Reports → Creditors.

Chapter 10

Tips & Common Mistakes

Things to know to avoid problems and use the app well every day.

✅ Good Daily Habits

  • Record every sale in the app — even if it is small. This keeps your stock accurate.
  • Record every purchase immediately when new goods arrive — so your stock goes up right away.
  • Run the End of Day report every evening to verify your totals.
  • Log out of the app when you leave your phone or computer unattended.
  • Check the Low Stock tab in Inventory at least twice a week so you don't run out unexpectedly.

❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake What to Do Instead
Recording a sale without the item in stock First record a purchase to add the stock, then sell it
Setting selling price lower than cost price Check the profit margin (it turns red) — raise your selling price
Forgetting to record when a customer pays back debt Always go to Payments → Customers after receiving any debt payment
Typing the wrong quantity Double-check before clicking Save. You can edit the sale afterwards if needed
Not adding customers before a credit sale Add the customer in CRM → Customers first, then record the sale
